Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

The consensus from helmet manufacturers is that the rider should replace their helmets every five years. That may seem like a lot, depending on how often you ride. This rule mostly applies to riders that hit the road every day. Riding for hours on end will only cause your helmet to degrade that much faster.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Check for Corrosion or Cracks Over the Entire Bike For that reason, you need to look over your entire bike for cracks when taking it out of storage. Plus, other parts of your bike like the spokes can corrode if stored near certain chemicals, so give your entire bike a good look-over before you take it out for a ride!

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Steer the bike where you want to go Look where you go and the bike will go where you look. Keeping your eyes up and looking ahead helps your body's inner-balance and direction. Looking down makes it harder to balance. Practice this while gliding along with your feet off the ground.
